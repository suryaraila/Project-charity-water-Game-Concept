# Game Concept Brainstorming Guide

## Provided Game Ideas

### 1. **Water Quest** ⚡ (Recommended for beginners)
**Style:** Whack-a-mole / Tap-to-collect  
**Mechanic:** Players tap falling yellow jerry cans to earn points

**Key Elements:**
- Score counter
- Timer (e.g., 60 seconds)
- Progressive difficulty (cans fall faster)
- Milestone feedback ("Great job! +50 pts")
- Optional obstacles (pollution drops to avoid)

**Why it works:**
- Simple to code (click detection, timer, score update)
- Satisfying feedback (tap = immediate response)
- Easy to scale difficulty
- Visual connection to charity: water's iconic yellow cans

---

### 2. **Water Drop** 💧 (Classic arcade style)
**Style:** Catch falling items  
**Mechanic:** Collect clean water droplets, avoid polluted drops

**Key Elements:**
- Score counter
- Lives system (3 lives?)
- Falling blue (clean) and black/red (polluted) drops
- Feedback messages on hits/misses
- Speed increases over time

**Why it works:**
- Teaches awareness of water quality
- Risk/reward gameplay (avoid bad drops)
- Clear visual distinction (color coding)
- Engaging penalty system (lose lives)

---

### 3. **Well Builder** 🏗️ (Strategy-puzzle hybrid)
**Style:** Building/collection puzzle  
**Mechanic:** Players collect resources to build wells in communities

**Key Elements:**
- Progress bar showing well construction
- Grid-based collection (match water resources)
- Community cards showing impact (X people now have clean water!)
- Milestone achievements
- Timer or turn-based system

**Why it works:**
- Meaningful connection to mission (building wells = impact)
- Engages college students in purpose-driven gameplay
- Scalable: add difficulty through resource complexity

---

### 4. **Hydration Hero** 🦸 (Adventure-style)
**Style:** Simple platformer or navigation game  
**Mechanic:** Guide a character to bring clean water to communities

**Key Elements:**
- Character movement (arrow keys or clicks)
- Obstacles to navigate
- Communities to reach with water
- Progress toward goal
- Score based on efficiency

**Why it works:**
- Narrative element (hero's journey)
- Visual progression
- Customizable difficulty
- Strong alignment with mission story

---

### 5. **Tap for Good** 💪 (Clicker-style)
**Style:** Rapid-click/incremental game  
**Mechanic:** Each tap contributes to providing clean water access

**Key Elements:**
- Big tap area
- Points per tap displayed
- Upgrades (tap more drops at once, faster tapping, multipliers)
- Community counter (X people helped)
- Achievements unlocked

**Why it works:**
- Super simple to code
- Addictive gameplay
- Mobile-friendly
- Clear impact visualization

---

## How to Choose Your Game

### Ask Yourself:

1. **What sounds FUN to you?**
   - Which concept would you enjoy playing?
   - Which would you enjoy building?

2. **What's your current skill level?**
   - Just starting? → Water Quest or Tap for Good
   - Getting comfortable? → Water Drop or Hydration Hero
   - Feeling confident? → Well Builder

3. **How much time do you have?**
   - 1-2 weeks → Simpler mechanics (Quest, Drop, Tap)
   - 2+ weeks → More complex (Hero, Well Builder)

4. **What excites you about the mission?**
   - Speed/reflexes? → Water Quest or Drop
   - Building/seeing impact? → Well Builder
   - Being a hero? → Hydration Hero
   - Community contribution? → Tap for Good
